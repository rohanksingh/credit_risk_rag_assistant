CECL Methodology Overview

The Current Expected Credit Loss (CECL) framework requires institutions to estimate lifetime credit losses at the time of loan origination.

Expected Credit Loss (ECL) is calculated using:
ECL = PD × LGD × EAD

Key Drivers:
- Probability of Default (PD)
- Loss Given Default (LGD)
- Exposure at Default (EAD)

Macroeconomic variables such as GDP growth, unemployment rate, and interest rates significantly impact PD estimates.

Under stressed scenarios, higher unemployment typically leads to increased PD, resulting in higher expected losses.
